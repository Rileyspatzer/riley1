Everyday Code

The Dear Data Project taught me alot about how I interact with technology and data throughout my daily life. I started by gathering a Weeks worth of Data from both by computer and my cellphone. I marked how many hours I spent on apps, how many times I opened an electronic, as well as how long I spent on an app per pick-up.I found that most of the weekdays I spent most of my time using school related applications, as well as listening to music. Typically, I only used socials and entertainment in the beginning and towards the end of the day. During the weekends,I saw how much later I was staying up and how I used social medias alot more frequently throughout the day. The data actually was refreshing to see, as I found that I balance my schoolwork and social medias well. I also went on my phone alot less that I actually thought I was - averaging around four hours a day. For me, this was a big improvement from how I used to be, which was pretty addicted to various social medias.

Code and digital technology impact on education

I think that digital technology and code has changed the way we learn entirely. Almost every academic function we use today is entirely digital. Other than optionaly writing notes in my notebook, I have found that so far my college education has been entirely online. Even textbooks are becoming fully available online. Personally, I've always been a fan of a physical book because I always absorb the information better that way. Additionally, AI is becoming an increasingly large factor in education today. It's definitely cruicial to consider the fact that younger and older students have access to the answer to pretty much any question they can ask through features like chat GPT. While this can be a helpful study tool, academic integrity and plagarism also come into question when this software is used. Either way, I think that code has definitely improved education as a whole; It just needs to be kept in check.
